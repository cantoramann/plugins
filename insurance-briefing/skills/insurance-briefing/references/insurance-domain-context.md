# Turkish Insurance Market: Domain Context Reference

This document encodes institutional knowledge about the Turkish insurance market. All agents in the insurance briefing pipeline reference this file to ground their analysis in accurate regulatory, competitive, and market context.

**Last Updated**: March 2026
**Scope**: Turkish insurance and pension markets

---

## 1. Regulatory Bodies

### SEDDK (Sigortacılık ve Özel Emeklilik Düzenleme ve Denetleme Kurumu)
- **English**: Insurance and Private Pension Regulation and Supervision Authority
- **Authority**: Regulates all insurance companies, brokers, and pension funds
- **Parent Ministry**: Hazine ve Maliye Bakanlığı (Ministry of Treasury and Finance)
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Licensing and supervision of insurers and brokers
  - Solvency and capital adequacy regulation
  - Policy consumer protection
  - Insurance law enforcement
- **Website**: seddk.gov.tr
- **Key Publications**:
  - Monthly insurance market statistics
  - Regulatory decisions and circulars
  - Solvency reports

### Hazine ve Maliye Bakanlığı (HMB)
- **English**: Ministry of Treasury and Finance
- **Role**: Parent ministry overseeing SEDDK
- **Key Function**: Sets macroeconomic policy affecting insurance markets

### TSB (Türkiye Sigorta Birliği)
- **English**: Insurance Association of Turkey
- **Type**: Industry trade association
- **Members**: All Turkish insurers
- **Key Activities**:
  - Annual market statistics publication (typically March)
  - Industry advocacy and standards
  - Professional development programs
- **Website**: tsb.org.tr

### DASK (Doğal Afet Sigortaları Kurumu)
- **English**: Turkish Catastrophe Insurance Pool
- **Role**: Provides compulsory earthquake (deprem sigortası) and natural disaster insurance
- **Mechanism**: Covers earthquake losses; ceded to international reinsurers above retention
- **Legal Status**: Mandatory for residential property insurance (for owner-occupied homes)
- **Website**: dask.org.tr

### Güvence Hesabı (Guarantee Fund)
- **Purpose**: Protects policyholders in case of insurer insolvency
- **Funding**: Contributions from all insurers
- **Coverage Limits**: Set by regulation for different line of business
- **Administration**: SEDDK oversight

### SBM (Sigorta Bilgi ve Gözetim Merkezi)
- **English**: Insurance Information and Monitoring Center
- **Role**: Centralized database of insurance claims and policyholders
- **Function**: Prevents fraud and tracks market dynamics
- **Data Sharing**: Available to SEDDK and member insurers

## 4. Common Turkish Insurance Acronyms & Terms

### Regulatory & Market Entities
- **SEDDK** — Sigortacılık ve Özel Emeklilik Düzenleme ve Denetleme Kurumu (regulator)
- **TSB** — Türkiye Sigorta Birliği (industry association)
- **DASK** — Doğal Afet Sigortaları Kurumu (earthquake pool)
- **SBM** — Sigorta Bilgi ve Gözetim Merkezi (claims/fraud database)
- **TSEV** — Türkiye Sigorta Enstitüsü Vakfı (insurance institute)

### Financial & Underwriting Metrics
- **Teknik kâr** — Technical profit (underwriting profit before investment income)
- **Hasar prim oranı** — Loss ratio (claims incurred / premiums earned)
- **Prim üretimi** — Premium production / premium written
- **Muallak hasar** — Outstanding claims / IBNR (Incurred But Not Reported)
- **Tazminat** — Claims payment / indemnity

### Reinsurance & Risk Transfer
- **Reasürans** — Reinsurance
- **Sedan** — Cedant (ceding insurer)
- **Trete** — Treaty reinsurance (quota share, excess of loss, etc.)
- **Fakultatif** — Facultative reinsurance (case-by-case)
- **Retrosesyon** — Retrocession (reinsurer ceding to another reinsurer)
- **CAT** — Catastrophe (usually earthquake in Turkish context)
- **ILS** — Insurance-linked securities (cat bonds, etc.)

### Pension & Life Insurance
- **BES** — Bireysel Emeklilik Sistemi (private/individual pension system)
- **OKS** — Otomatik Katılım Sistemi (automatic enrollment system for pensions)
- **Hayat Sigortası** — Life insurance

### Product Lines (Common Usage)
- **Deprem Sigortası** — Earthquake insurance (DASK-backed)
- **MTPL / Kasko** — Motor third-party liability & comprehensive motor insurance
- **Yangın** — Fire insurance
- **Sağlık** — Health insurance
- **Sorumluluk** — Liability insurance

## 7. Calendar & Cadence

### Quarterly Events
- **Quarterly Earnings** — 15th of month following quarter end (April, July, Oct, Jan)
  - Companies announce results and discuss market conditions
  - BIST-listed insurers file with SEDDK within 15 days of quarter end
- **SEDDK Monthly Reports** — Published mid-month with prior month's market data
  - Premium volumes by insurer and line of business
  - Claims and loss ratios
  - Market concentration statistics

### Annual Events
- **TSB Annual Statistics** — Published typically in March
  - Comprehensive market overview for prior calendar year
  - Premium distribution by line and company
  - Industry profitability analysis
  - Projections for current year
- **SEDDK Annual Report** — Published by May
  - Regulatory actions and policy updates
  - Market health and solvency assessments
  - Consumer complaint analysis

### Seasonal/Market Events
- **1 January & 1 July** — Treaty reinsurance renewal dates
  - Major reinsurance placements tied to these dates
  - Capacity and pricing announcements expected
  - DASK annual coverage review typically aligned
- **DASK Premium & Coverage Reviews** — Annual (usually Q1)
  - Earthquake insurance premium rate adjustments announced
  - Pool coverage limits and retention reviewed
- **SEDDK Circular Releases** — Year-round, typically 2-4 per month
  - Regulatory guidance on new rules, solvency requirements, consumer protection
  - Often trigger industry compliance timelines

### Market Activity Patterns
- **Slow periods** — June-August (summer), December 25-31 (holidays)
- **High activity** — September-November, January-February (new fiscal planning, renewals)

## 8. Key Regulations & Concepts

### Solvency & Capital
- **Minimum Capital Requirements** — Set by SEDDK based on insurer class and risk profile
- **Solvency II Framework** — EU regulations increasingly influential in Turkish requirements
- **Premium Production Limits** — Insurers must maintain adequate capital relative to premium written

### Consumer Protection
- **Policy Language** — All policies must be in Turkish
- **Clear Terms** — Standard policy conditions must be pre-approved by SEDDK
- **Complaint Resolution** — SEDDK oversees complaints process; annual data published

### Claims & Proof of Loss
- **Suret** — Certified copy of original document
- **Kesin Hesap** — Final accounting/settlement statement
- **Hasar Dosyası** — Claims file/claim dossier

## 9. Quick Reference: Market Structure

### Non-Life (Composite) Insurance Market
- **Estimated Market Size**: ~30-35 billion TL annual premiums (2025)
- **Concentration**: Top 5 companies ~50% market share
- **Main Products**: Motor (MTPL/Kasko), Fire, Liability, Marine, Engineering, Misc.
- **Growth Driver**: Motor insurance (mandatory MTPL), construction growth

### Life & Pension Market
- **Estimated Market Size**: ~8-12 billion TL (2025)
- **Concentration**: Anadolu Hayat Emeklilik dominates (~25-30% pension market)
- **Growth Driver**: Auto-enrollment in BES (private pensions) since 2017
- **Regulatory Trend**: Pushing savings into pension system; life insurance secondary

### Reinsurance Market Access
- **Treaty Placements**: Major Turkish insurers access global reinsurance through brokers (Marsh, Aon, WTW)
- **Retrocession**: Large reinsurers then place Turkish exposure with Lloyd's and global markets
- **Typical Structure**: Layers of excess-of-loss treaties + quota-share for catastrophe

## 10. Agent Reference Guide

### For Scout Agents
- Use TSB and SEDDK as primary authoritative sources for market data
- Cross-check premium volumes with monthly SEDDK reports
- For company-specific news, check Turkish business press (Dünya, Bloomberg HT)
- DASK announcements critical for earthquake insurance / CAT analysis
- Reinsurance treaty news from Artemis and broker announcements

### For Composer Agent
- Ground narrative in regulatory data and official TSB/SEDDK reports
- Distinguish between company guidance and analyst estimates
- BIST tickers useful for investor perspective; cite specific earnings announcements
- Calendar-aware: note if analysis period includes critical event (earnings, reinsurance renewal, etc.)
- Always ground geopolitical or macroeconomic claims in Turkish-specific insurance impact

### For Cross-Pollinator Agent
- Insurance market often influenced by inflation (premium adjustments), interest rates (investment returns), geopolitics (CAT risk)
- Link Turkish macroeconomic data to insurance impact (e.g., lira depreciation → higher reinsurance costs)
- BES growth tied to confidence in Turkish financial system
- Cross-reference with broader Turkish economic briefing for context
