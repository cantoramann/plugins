# Briefing Structure Specification

The final briefing is a single markdown file with the following sections.

## File Naming

`briefing-YYYY-MM-DD.md` (e.g., `briefing-2026-03-07.md`)

## Header

```markdown
# Sigorta Brifing — [Günü], [Tarih]
```

Example: `# Sigorta Brifing — Cuma, 7 Mart 2026`

## Section 1: Gündem (Agenda)

4-6 headline items covering the most consequential stories of the day across insurance domains.

```markdown
## Gündem

### [Başlık 1]
[One paragraph: what happened + sharp take on insurance implications. 2-4 sentences.]

### [Başlık 2]
...
```

**Selection criteria:**
- At least 2 different insurance domains represented (regulatory, market, industry, cross-domain)
- Lead with the single most consequential story
- Each item must pass the Practitioner Test
- Order by impact on insurance professionals, not by source

## Section 2: Derinlemesine (Deep Dives)

2-3 medium-depth articles. Each is a self-contained piece with its own title.

```markdown
## Derinlemesine

### [Başlık: Açıklayıcı, Clickbait Değil]

[300-500 words. Structure: Context → News → Analysis → Insurance Implication.]

---

### [Başlık]

[300-500 words.]
```

**Selection criteria:**
- Stories that deserve more than a headline but less than a full deep dive
- Must add genuine analysis beyond what a headline conveys
- Prioritize stories where the "why" and "what it means" are more interesting than the "what"
- Clear connection to underwriting, pricing, reserving, investment, or strategy

## Section 3: Radar

1-2 pieces about non-obvious insurance mechanisms hidden in seemingly non-insurance news.

```markdown
## Radar

### [Başlık]

[400-700 words per item. Structure: The news story → The insurance mechanism → Why it matters → What to watch.]
```

**Selection criteria:**
- Must name the insurance mechanism explicitly — not vague implications
- Non-insurance news with concrete transmission to insurance decision-making
- Global-to-local CAT connections, regulatory echoes, hidden cost pressures, demand signals
- High intellectual rigor — reader finishes understanding something new about how insurance works

## Section 4: Gelişmeler (Developments) — Conditional

Short updates on stories from previous briefings that have material new developments. Only include this section if there are genuine updates on previously covered stories. Skip entirely if there are no continuity updates.

```markdown
## Gelişmeler

### [Story Headline] — Güncelleme
[2-4 sentences. Structure: "Dün bahsettiğimiz [Tarih] tarihinde [durum]. O zamandan beri, [ne değişti]." Direct, no re-explaining the full backstory — the reader already knows it.]

### [Story Headline] — Güncelleme
[2-4 sentences.]
```

**Selection criteria:**
- Only stories with material new developments (regulatory action, new data, significant market move, resolution)
- Maximum 3 updates per briefing — if more exist, pick the most consequential
- Each update must add genuinely new information, not restate what was covered before
- Frame as progression: "Dün bahsettiğimiz..." or "X gelişmesi..."

## Section 5: Küresel Reasürans (Global Reinsurance)

2-3 brief items on international insurance and reinsurance developments with explicit Turkish relevance.

```markdown
## Küresel Reasürans

### [Global Development + Turkish Connection]
[2-3 sentences. The global situation + explicit relevance to Turkish market or DASK.]

### [Item]
[2-3 sentences.]
```

**Selection criteria:**
- International reinsurance market moves, global insurer strategy shifts, international CAT developments
- Must explicitly state Turkish relevance — don't leave the reader inferring
- CAT events, reinsurance pricing trends, international regulatory changes affecting Turkish market
- Keep international focus but always translate to Turkish context

## Footer

```markdown
---

*Generated by Insurance Briefing — a multi-agent curation system for Turkish insurance professionals. Sources searched broadly across Turkish and international publications.*
```

## Frontmatter Requirements

Each briefing file must include YAML frontmatter:

```yaml
---
title: "Sigorta Brifing — [Başlık]"
date: YYYY-MM-DD
language: tr
readingTime: 12
author: "Sigorta Brifing"
---
```

Example:
```yaml
---
title: "Sigorta Brifing — SEDDK Yeni Yönergesi ve Prim Trendleri"
date: 2026-03-07
language: tr
readingTime: 12
author: "Sigorta Brifing"
---
```
